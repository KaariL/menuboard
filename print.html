A Note on Timing

  • Make the dashi in advance, or while you are prepping, it is needed in almost all of the recipes.
  • Traditonally miso soup and edamame are server as starters, so those can completed first and served before moving onto the main.
  • Start the fish when potatoes are half cooked so they are finished together as main dish.
  • Please do not a white fish for this meal, unless it is extremly fresh. The flesh is far too delicate for the robust sauce.
  • Pair with Sake, a japanese rice wine.

Dashi(Fish Broth)

Cook time

35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1.5 L Water + 1/2 cup
  • 45 cm Kombu
  • 20g Katsuobushi (hana katsuo)

Instructions

  1. Wipe kombu with damp kitchen paper
  2. Break kombu into water on medium heat
  3. Simmer 20 mins; do not boil
  4. Remove kombu, bring to boil
  5. Immediatly shock with 1/2 cup of cold water so it is no longer at a boil
  6. Add katsuo, remove from heat do not boil
  7. Let stand 10 minutes or until katsuo sinks
  8. Skim foam do not skip this step
  9. Strain

Miso Soup

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

10 minutes

Ingredients

  • 700 mL Dashi
  • 1 Green Onion
  • 1/4 Block Soft Tofu
  • 1/2 tbs Dry Wakame
  • 1 1/2 tbs Shiro Miso(white miso)
  • 1 tbs Soy Sauce

Instructions

  1. Slice tofu into 1cm cubes
  2. Warm dashi almost to boil
  3. Make sure dashi is not boiling, push miso through sieve into broth.
  4. Portion tofu and wakame in serving bowls. Then, add broth
  5. Garnish with sliced green onions, serve

Dashi-Braised New Potatos with Onion

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 Onion
  • 300 g New Potatos
  • 600 mL Dashi
  • 1 tbs Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbs Mirin
  • 1 tbs Each Canola Oil and Sesame Oil
  • Garnish

Instructions

  1. Slice onion into thin rings
  2. Heat saucepan to medium-high
  3. Add oils and onion
  4. Cook until onions just until transparent(this is known as sweating)
  5. Add potatoes
  6. Cook until onions are brown
  7. Add dashi, boil, then reduce heat to low and add lid
  8. Cook 14 mins or until tender
  9. Remove lid and reduce sauce until thickly coating
  10. Salt and pepper to taste, garnish and serve

Edamame

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

7 minutes

Ingredients

  • Whole, Fresh Edamame Beans
  • 2 tbs White Vinegar
  • 2 tbs Sake
  • Coarse Salt

Instructions

  1. Bring saucepan of water, vinegar and sake to a boil
  2. steam edamame 6 mins or until bright green
  3. season with salt and serve

Teriyaki Mackeral

Prep Time

8 minutes

Cook time

12 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 Fish Gutted, Scaled, Head and Tailed
  • 2 Clove Garlic
  • 1 Shallot(or Half Onion)
  • 1 tbs Each Canola Oil and Sesame oil
  • 2 tbs Soy Sauce
  • 4 tbs Each Dashi and Mirin
  • 250 mL Flour(White or Rice)
  • Optional: Shichimi Togarashii (or Ground Cayenne Pepper)
  • Squeeze of Lemon

Instructions

  1. Bring medium saucepan to medium high heat
  2. Add onions and fry
  3. Season the flour with salt and pepperdo not skip
  4. Add garlic once the onion is transluscent(this is called sweating the onion)
  5. Dredge fish in flour mixture
  6. Add fish to pan and brown both sides
  7. Add soy sauce, dashi and mirin
  8. Cook until the sauce is thick and syropy
  9. Add salt and pepper to taste, lemon and serve
