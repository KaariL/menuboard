Dashi(Fish Broth)

This simple and delicate broth proves invaluable in the kitchen, we use it in nearly all of the following recipes, and can be frozen for convenient use.

Cook time

35 minutes

Yield

1 Litre

Ingredients

  • 1 L water + 1/2 cup
  • 30 cm Kombu
  • 200g Katsuobushi (hana katsuo)

Instructions

  1. Wipe kombu with damp kitchen paper
  2. Break kombu into water on medium heat
  3. Simmer 20 mins; do not boil
  4. Remove kombu, bring to boil
  5. Immediatly shock with 1/2 cup of cold water so it is no longer at a boil
  6. Add katsuo, remove from heat do not boil
  7. Let stand 10 minutes or until katsuo sinks
  8. skim foam do not skip this step
  9. strain

Miso Soup

Soft enough for any season, and yet the gentle spice of the fermented soybean paste(miso) makes for a hearty and warming dish

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

EDIT

Yield

1 Litre

Ingredients

  • 1 L dashi
  • 2 greens onions
  • 1/2 block soft tofu
  • 2 tbs dry wakame
  • 2 tbs shiro miso(white miso)

Instructions

  1. Slice tofu into 1cm cubes
  2. Slice green onion on hard bias
  3. do that while warming dashi almost to boil
  4. Add tofu, wakame, miso(special instruction here)
  5. garnish with green onions, serve

Dashi-Braised New Potatos with Onions

While new potatos provide something special that sam will tell me, they can easily be substituted with cut up potatos. Such dashi, very onion

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

30 minutes

Yield

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 400 g new potatos
  • 500 mL dashi
  • 1 tbs each canola oil and sesame oil
  • garnish

Instructions

  1. slice onion into thin rings
  2. Heat suacepan to medium-high
  3. Add oils and onion
  4. cook until onions transparent(this is known as sweating)
  5. add new potatoes
  6. cook until onions are brown
  7. add dashi, boil, then reduce heat to low and add lid
  8. cooks 10 mins or until tender
  9. remove lid and reduce sauce until coating
  10. salt and pepper to taste, garnish and serve

Edamame with Salt

steamed to life, vibrant green and coarse salt a must. dont bite into that pod dummy, just leck them beans out into your belly. We use rice vinegar to season the steaming liquid

Prep Time

5 minutes

Cook time

5 minutes

Yield

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • whole, fresh edamame beans
  • 2 tbs white vinegar
  • 2 tbs Sake
  • coarse salt

Instructions

  1. Bring saucepan of water, vinegar and sake to a boil
  2. steam edamame 4 mins or until bright green
  3. season with salt and serve

Teriyaki Mackerel

Bold authentic quality. sweetness spicyness delisiousness. use this recipe to wina cookoff with the devil

Prep Time

DUNNO

Cook time

DUNNO

Yield

Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 1 fish gutted, scaled, head and tailed
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 shallot(or half onion)
  • 1 tbs each canola oil and sesame oil
  • 1 tbs soy sauce
  • 2tbs each dashi and mirin
  • 250 mL flour(white or rice)
  • optional: shichimi togarashii (or ground cayenne pepper)
  • garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring medium saucepan to medium high heat
  2. add oilions and fry
  3. season flour with salt and pepperdo not skip
  4. add garlic when onion is transluscent
  5. dredge fish in flour mixture
  6. add fish to pan, brown both sides
  7. add soy sauce, dashi and mirin
  8. cook until sauce thick and syropy
  9. salt and pepper to taste, garnish and serve
